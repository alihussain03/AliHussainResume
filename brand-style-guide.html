The relationship

One accent. Two voices.

Both sites share everything — neutrals, type, spacing, and a single deep-petrol accent. They differ only in register: the personal site is quieter and first-person; the studio site is more structured. Same brand, felt two ways. This keeps them effortless to keep consistent.

Keeping it consistent

The rules that stop two sites from quietly drifting apart.

Do
  • Use the exact tokens from this file — never eyeball a value
  • Let neutrals lead; reach for petrol only with intent
  • Reuse the same button, tag, and card on both sites
  • Protect the whitespace — air is what reads as premium
Don't
  • Add a second accent or a "close enough" shade
  • Introduce a third font, or swap the serif for a trend
  • Use random spacing — stay on the 8px steps
  • Add gradients, glows, or effects not defined here
